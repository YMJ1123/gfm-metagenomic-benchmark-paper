\subsection{Preliminary validation on a real mock community}
\label{sec:mock}

All results so far use simulated reads. As a first check on real data we ran the
trained genus classifiers (inference only) on real Illumina reads from the
ZymoBIOMICS Gut Microbiome Standard (D6331; run SRR33710519, $\sim$3M reads,
median length 135\,bp), whose theoretical genomic-DNA composition is the
sample-level ground truth. Of the 21 mock taxa, 11 genera (81.5\% of the
community) fall within our 120 training genera; the remaining 18.5\% are
out-of-set (dominated by \emph{Escherichia} at 14\%, which has no counterpart
among the 120 genera) and therefore cannot be classified correctly---an
unavoidable ceiling for this mock.

On the 11 in-set genera the neural models' genus-abundance correlation drops
sharply from the closed-set $r\approx0.99$ to $r=0.55$ (MT 13-mer) and $r=0.42$
(NT-v2 6-mer), with detection sensitivity $8/11$ at a 1\% threshold. This drop is
expected---real error profiles, a 135\,bp vs 150\,bp length mismatch, out-of-set
taxa, and a single sample ($n=11$ in-set genera, so the MT\,$>$\,NT ordering is
not itself conclusive)---and it directly illustrates the closed-set-to-real gap
that our simulated numbers cannot capture.
