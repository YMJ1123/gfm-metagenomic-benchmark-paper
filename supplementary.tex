\section{Is 13-mer just lookup? Non-neural baselines}
\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{$k$-mer classifiers on the same 50M-read reference and test pool, with
a 6-mer control that isolates $k$-mer length as the only variable. Genus Top-1
accuracy (\%). Holding the method fixed, naive Bayes on 13-mer ($74.9$) is
$\sim$3$\times$ that on 6-mer ($25.9$); the neural gain is larger on the weaker
6-mer tokenizer ($+41$ pp) than on 13-mer ($+13$ pp); and the non-neural
13-mer naive Bayes ($74.9$) exceeds the 498M-parameter NT-v2 on 6-mer ($67$).
s1 = overlapping (stride 1); s6 = non-overlapping (stride 6, the NT-v2 setting).}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}Xccc@{}}
\toprule
Method & 6-mer (s1) & 6-mer (s6) & 13-mer \\
\midrule
Unique-key lookup (Kraken-style) & 0.00 & 0.00 & 0.72 \\
Dominant-genus majority vote & 30.7 & 30.7 & 35.3 \\
Multinomial naive Bayes & 25.7 & 25.9 & 74.9 \\
Neural network & --- & 67.0\textsuperscript{a} & 87.4\textsuperscript{b} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\vspace{2pt}
{\footnotesize \textsuperscript{a}498M-parameter pre-trained NT-v2 + LoRA.\quad
\textsuperscript{b}MetaTransformer, from scratch.}
\end{table}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.82\linewidth]{kmer_baselines.pdf}
\caption{Genus Top-1 accuracy with the classifier held fixed (non-neural
multinomial $k$-mer naive Bayes) or as a neural network, for 6-mer versus
overlapping 13-mer tokenization on the same 50M-read reference and test pool.
Holding the method fixed, moving to 13-mer nearly triples accuracy
($25.9\%\!\rightarrow\!74.9\%$), isolating $k$-mer length as the decisive
variable; the non-neural 13-mer naive Bayes (74.9\%) already exceeds the
498M-parameter NT-v2 on 6-mer (67\%). Exact-match unique-key lookup fails
(0.7\%), confirming learned composition rather than lookup.}
\end{figure}

\section{Real mock community (D6331) details}
\label{sec:supp-mock}

Ground truth is the vendor \textbf{Genomic DNA \%} table (appropriate for
shotgun read-count abundance). Evaluation uses 3M R1 reads per replicate
(median 135\,bp); Bracken was run with \texttt{-r 150} against the custom
catalogue database (only a 150-mer distribution was built). Abundance metrics
renormalize predicted and expected fractions over the 11 in-set genera.
Detection sensitivity counts a genus as detected if its renormalized predicted
fraction is $\ge$1\%, restricted to genera with expected abundance $\ge$1\%
(nine genera). Pearson bootstrap CIs resample the 11 genus pairs ($B{=}5000$).
